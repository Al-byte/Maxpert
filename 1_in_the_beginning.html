Creation and The Goodness of God

In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth, and when darkness covered the deep, He spoke light into existence, separating it from the darkness and calling them Day and Night. He then formed the expanse of the sky, dividing the waters above from the waters below. Gathering the waters together, He caused dry land to appear, naming it Earth and the gathered waters Seas, and He clothed the land with vegetation, plants, and fruit-bearing trees. Next, He set the sun, moon, and stars in the heavens to give light upon the earth and to mark seasons, days, and years. He filled the seas with living creatures and the skies with winged birds, blessing them to be fruitful and multiply. He then created the animals of the land and formed mankind in His own image, male and female, granting them dominion over all the earth. And when His work was complete, God rested on the seventh day, blessing and sanctifying it as holy.
