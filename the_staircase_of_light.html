The Trials of Emberfall: Rite of the Rising

The Staircase of Light

The maze fell away, replaced by a vast vertical chamber filled with golden haze. Suspended within it was a spiraling staircase of stone steps—yet many of them flickered, appearing and vanishing like illusions. There were no rails. No visible support. Words shimmered in the air, glowing softly but unmistakably:

Trust what you cannot see.

Maximus hesitated at the edge. This trial offered no pattern to memorize, no obstacle to reason through. It demanded belief. He stepped forward. For a breathless moment, there was nothing—then solid stone formed beneath his foot. Another step. Another stone. With each upward movement, his confidence grew. Fear loosened its grip, replaced by quiet confidence. He climbed higher, trusting not his eyes, but his purpose.

At the top of the staircase stood a heavy oak door carved with ancient symbols, worn smooth by generations of hands. Maximus placed his palm against it, steady and sure, and pushed it open.
