The Trials of Emberfall: Rite of the Rising

The Beast of Roots

The forest floor cracked open with a thunderous sound, roots snapping like bones as something enormous forced its way upward. Vines twisted together, thickening into limbs, while bark hardened into plated armor. Before Maximus stood a towering beast formed entirely of roots and soil, its eyes glowing an unnatural green. The creature roared, a sound that vibrated through Maximus’ chest, and charged.

Every instinct screamed at him to flee. His hand twitched toward the small blade at his side. Fighting would prove his strength. Running would save his life. Both were reasonable. Both were expected. But his father’s teaching rose above the fear: Bravery isn’t just facing danger—it’s choosing the right action in the face of it. Maximus planted his feet and raised his hands, forcing himself to remain still as the beast loomed over him.

“I won’t harm you,” he said, his voice shaking but firm. “I honor this forest. Let me pass in peace.”

For a long moment, nothing happened. Then the beast slowed. Its vines trembled, the glow in its eyes dimming. Slowly, deliberately, it lowered its massive head. With a deep, earthen groan, the creature unraveled, sinking back into the soil as if it had never existed.

Maximus staggered back, heart pounding, awe replacing fear. Compassion had proven stronger than force. He continued onward, forever changed by the choice he had made.
